Privacy Policy

My Six Degrees  ·  Effective Date: June 5, 2026  ·  Last Updated: June 9, 2026

My Six Degrees is built with your privacy in mind. We collect only what is necessary to power your network, we never sell your data, and we never use it for advertising.

1. Who We Are

My Six Degrees is operated by Ryan Pelletier ("we", "us", or "our"), an individual developer based in the United States. If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, you can contact us at rdp4709@gmail.com.

2. What Information We Collect

When you create an account, we collect the following information that you provide directly:

  • Full name — displayed on your profile card within the network map
  • Occupation — displayed on your profile card
  • Postal code — used to place your node on the geographic map
  • Bio — a short description you write about yourself

We also automatically derive and store:

  • Approximate geographic coordinates — derived from your postal code using OpenStreetMap's Nominatim geocoding service. We do not collect your precise GPS location.
  • Connection relationships — records of who referred who, used to build your network map
  • Account creation timestamp — the date and time you joined

3. What We Do Not Collect

  • We do not collect your precise GPS or real-time location
  • We do not access your contacts, camera, microphone, or photos
  • We do not collect payment card details — all payments are processed by Apple
  • We do not use advertising tracking or third-party ad networks
  • We do not sell your personal information to any third party

4. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect solely to operate and improve My Six Degrees:

  • To display your profile on the network map for your connections
  • To calculate and visualize your degrees of separation from other users
  • To generate your personalized network snapshot file
  • To send you app updates and important service communications
  • To diagnose technical issues and improve app performance

5. How We Store Your Information

Your data is stored securely using Google Firebase, a cloud platform provided by Google LLC. Your profile data is stored in Firebase Firestore, and your network snapshot is stored in Google Cloud Storage. Both services are hosted in the United States.

Google's privacy policy is available at policies.google.com/privacy.

6. Third-Party Services

My Six Degrees uses the following third-party services to operate:

  • Google Firebase — database, cloud functions, and file storage
  • OpenStreetMap Nominatim — postal code to coordinate geocoding. Only your postal code is sent to this service. No personal identifying information is transmitted.
  • Apple App Store — payment processing for in-app purchases. We never receive or store your payment details.
  • Firebase Crashlytics — anonymous crash reporting to help us fix bugs

7. Payments

My Six Degrees is free to download and use. Optional in-app purchases are available and are processed entirely by Apple through the App Store. We do not receive, store, or process any payment card information. Apple's privacy policy governs how your payment data is handled and is available at apple.com/legal/privacy.

8. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. Your profile information (name, occupation, and approximate location) is visible to other users within the app as part of the network map experience. This is core to the functionality of My Six Degrees.

We may disclose your information if required to do so by law or in response to valid legal requests by public authorities.

9. Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as your account is active. If you wish to delete your account and all associated data, please contact us at rdp4709@gmail.com and we will process your request within 30 days.

10. Children's Privacy

My Six Degrees is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

11. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access — request a copy of the data we hold about you
  • Correction — request that we correct inaccurate data
  • Deletion — request that we delete your account and data
  • Portability — request your data in a portable format

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at rdp4709@gmail.com.

12. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA). You have the right to know what personal information we collect, to request deletion of your personal information, and to opt out of the sale of your personal information. We do not sell personal information. To exercise your rights, contact us at rdp4709@gmail.com.

13. GDPR — European Users

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or Switzerland, the following applies to you in addition to the rights described above.

Legal Basis for Processing
We process your personal data on the following legal bases:

  • Contractual necessity — to provide the app's core functionality
  • Legitimate interests — to maintain app security and prevent abuse
  • Consent — where you have explicitly agreed to data collection

Your Additional Rights Under GDPR
In addition to the rights listed in Section 11, you have the right to:

  • Object to processing — object to our processing of your personal data
  • Restrict processing — request that we limit how we use your data
  • Withdraw consent — at any time, where processing is based on consent
  • Lodge a complaint — with your local data protection authority if you believe we have not handled your data in accordance with GDPR

We will respond to all data rights requests within 30 days.

Data Transfers
Your data may be transferred to and processed in the United States. We use Google Firebase infrastructure, which provides adequate data protection safeguards. For more information on Google's data transfer mechanisms, see policies.google.com/privacy.

14. Security

We take reasonable technical measures to protect your personal information, including secure cloud storage, access controls, and encrypted data transmission. However, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

15. Changes to This Policy
